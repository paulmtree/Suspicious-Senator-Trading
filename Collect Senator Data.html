Using this graph we can observe there are several dates in which an usually high number of trades were placed in one day. These days could indicate suspicious activity due to insider information being released. On the other hand they could also be days when impactful news was released or other non-insider sources.

It may be useful to examine these trade days to see if there is a a buildup or some other unusual activity.

from datetime import datetime, timedelta
def graph_range(center, freq_df = trade_freq_df, width = 10):
    glanceback = timedelta(days= width)
    low = center - glanceback
    high = center + glanceback
    range_df = freq_df[low:high]
    
    range_df.Count.plot(title = str(center.strftime("%B, %d, %Y")))
    plt.ylabel('Count')
    plt.show()

Quick Analysis¶

As we can see these top 10 dates have no precursor for these high volume trade dates. If we were to examine these specific dates we may well find news updates that prompt these high number of trades. Then again, one senator could be making these high number of trades or a number of of other scenarios that are not indicative of insider trading. While an interesting avenue for future analysis, it is not the direction we will be going.
